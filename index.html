You may be wondering what acting has to do with UX/UI design and front end development. I don’t blame you. After all, I probably am the first of my kind you’ve ever seen; but hey, there’s a first time for everything right.

I started the first five years of my educational journey in conservatories, learning the same acting methods as Marlon Brando, Stella Adler, Timothee Chalamet, and Jennifer Aniston to name a few. What did this teach me about design and development. Well for starters, it’s taught me enhanced communication allowing me to get to the root of any problem, and proactively find a solution faster and more efficiently. It’s taught me the how to collaborate with diverse personalities as well as how to give and receive feedback, a skill imperative for tech and dev teams alike. And it’s taught me how to storytelling with my code and designs. And let’s be honest, no-one wants a boring, one demential website.

So if you’re looking for someone who’s great to collab with, a dynamic problem solver, and who can create creative comprehensive website, you’re looking in the right direction.
